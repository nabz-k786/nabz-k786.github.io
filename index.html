Multiplication of Integers
Sign Rules

Why is a negative times a negative a positive?
Subject Mathematics
Class VII — NCERT, Chapter: Integers

Screens 8 screens
Approach Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract (CPA)

1
The Hook — Rohan's Monthly Debt
Concrete Stage
Page Overview
Hook

Introduce the need for integer multiplication through a familiar real-life story — a monthly debt — before any formal symbol appears. The goal is to make students feel the problem before they solve it.

Visual Description
Background: Soft off-white, clean. No clutter or decorative elements.
Left side — Character: A cartoon boy named Rohan. Simple two-colour flat design. He holds an empty wallet (open, flat rectangle outline). His expression is mildly worried — a simple downward-curved mouth arc, eyebrows slightly raised. Label "Rohan" below in grey text.
Right side — Three debt cards: Three identical rectangular cards placed side by side (horizontal). Each card is coral-red fill with a dark-red 1px border. Each shows "₹50" in bold white text (centre). A small tab below each card reads "Month 1," "Month 2," "Month 3" in grey. Cards are initially at full opacity (all visible).
Top-right corner: A small pill badge — dark green text on light green background — reading "Real-life situation".
Bottom centre: A running total counter (hidden initially, appears after first tap): "Total debt: ₹___" in large text. A "Continue →" button below it — disabled/greyed out until all 3 cards are tapped.
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
Rohan has to pay ₹50 every month.

How much does he owe after 3 months?		 "Rohan ko har mahine ₹50 dena padta hai. Agar yeh 3 mahine tak chale, toh total kitna hua?"(Rohan has to pay ₹50 every month. If this continues for 3 months, what is the total?)
Tap each month's card to count the debt. "Har mahine ka card tap karo — dekhte hain kaise debt badh raha hai."(Tap each month's card — let us see how the debt grows.)
Can we find the total in one calculation instead of adding again and again? "Kya hum teen baar 50 jodne ki jagah ek hi step mein total nikaal sakte hain?"(Can we find the total in one step instead of adding 50 three times?)
Interaction Detail
  • Student
    Taps the "Month 1" card.
  • System
    Card glows with a 2px amber border. Counter appears at bottom: "Total debt: ₹50".
  • Student
    Taps "Month 2" card.
  • System
    Second card glows. Counter updates to "Total debt: ₹100".
  • Student
    Taps "Month 3" card.
  • System
    Third card glows. Counter updates to "Total debt: ₹150". The "Continue →" button activates (turns green). A brief ✓ animation plays over the counter.
  • Student
    Taps "Continue →" to proceed.
Feedback Logic
If tapped out of order
Cards must be tapped in sequence (Month 1 → 2 → 3). An out-of-order tap causes a soft shake animation on that card and shows a tooltip: "Start with Month 1 first."
All 3 tapped correctly
Green tick (✓) on all three cards. "Continue →" button activates with a brief green pulse. No extra text — let the momentum carry forward.
2
Pattern Discovery — Products Going Down
Concrete → Pictorial
Page Overview
Concept Building

Build the first half of the pattern table. Students watch products decrease steadily as the multiplier drops from 3 to 0, then predict the next values for negative multipliers. This creates the "what comes next?" moment before negative multipliers are introduced formally.

Visual Description
Title above table: "Let us look at a pattern." in bold purple text.
Table — 4 columns: "Sr. No. | Expression | Product | Sign of Product". Table has a purple header row (light purple fill, dark purple text). Column widths: 60px | 180px | 120px | 140px.
Rows 1–4 (3×4=12, 2×4=8, 1×4=4, 0×4=0): Soft green fill (#edf6e5). Sign column: a small rounded pill badge — + Positive for the first three, and a grey "zero" pill for row 4.
Rows 5–7 ((−1)×4, (−2)×4, (−3)×4): These rows are visible but greyed out (30% opacity) — locked. They will be revealed one by one as the student answers correctly.
Right side of table: A vertical dashed purple arrow running alongside the Product column, labeled "Each product decreases by 4 ↓".
Below table: A fill-in box: "Next product will be: ___" with a number keypad or text input. A "Check" button (green outline). This activates before each hidden row is revealed.
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
Look at this table carefully.

As the multiplier goes down by 1, what happens to the product?		 "Yeh table dhyan se dekho. Jab multiplier ek ek ghatta hai, toh product mein kya badlaav aata hai?"(Look at this table carefully. When the multiplier decreases by 1, what change happens in the product?)
3×4=12 → 2×4=8 → 1×4=4 → 0×4=0.

The product goes down by 4 each time.		 "12, 8, 4, 0 — product har baar 4 se ghatt raha hai. Yeh ek pattern hai."(12, 8, 4, 0 — the product decreases by 4 each time. This is a pattern.)
Now predict: What will (−1) × 4 be?
Type your answer in the box.		 "Ab batao: agar pattern aage bhi chale, toh (−1) × 4 kya hoga?"(Now tell me: if the pattern continues, what will (−1) × 4 be?)
Interaction Detail
  • System
    Rows 1–4 auto-animate in one by one (200ms fade-in per row) as the voiceover plays.
  • Student
    Reads the pattern. Fill-in box appears: "Next product: ___".
  • Student
    Types a number (expected: −4) and taps "Check".
  • System
    If correct: Row 5 fades in with a green glow (opacity 100%). Sign pill shows − Negative. A "Reveal Next" button appears.
  • Student
    Repeats the same predict-and-reveal process for (−2)×4 and (−3)×4.
  • System
    After all three rows are revealed, "Continue →" button activates.
Feedback Logic
Attempt 1 — Wrong
"Pattern dekho — product har baar kitna ghatt raha hai?"
(Look at the pattern — by how much is the product decreasing each time?)
The Product column pulses yellow to draw attention to the decreasing values.
Attempt 2 — Wrong
"0 × 4 = 0 tha. Isse 4 aur ghata do." (0 × 4 was 0. Subtract 4 more from it.)
Show the scaffold step: "0 − 4 = ___" for the student to fill in.
Attempt 3 — Wrong
Auto-reveal the answer (−4) with animation. Voiceover: "Yeh −4 hai — kyunki pattern ne 4 ghataane ko kaha." Move to the next hidden row automatically. No score penalty at this discovery stage.
Correct Answer
A small star-burst animation plays on the row. A toast pops for 1.5 seconds: "Well done!" Proceed to the next hidden row.
3
Number Line — Taking Jumps to the Left
Pictorial Stage
Page Overview
Concept Building

Give students a spatial understanding of positive × negative using directed jumps on a number line. Each leftward jump represents one step into negative territory. Students control the jumps themselves, building physical intuition before the abstract rule is stated.

Visual Description
Full-width number line: Spans −12 (left) to +3 (right). Bold horizontal baseline (2px). Tick marks at every 3 units, each labeled. Arrowheads at both ends pointing outward.
Start marker: A filled green circle (radius 10px) at 0. Label below: "Start here" in green text.
Three jump arcs (drawn above the number line, rainbow-arc style): Arc 1 (lightest coral, 0 → −4), Arc 2 (medium coral, −4 → −8), Arc 3 (darkest coral, −8 → −12). Each arc is labeled above it: "Jump 1 (−4)", "Jump 2 (−4)", "Jump 3 (−4)". Arcs appear one at a time when the student taps the jump button.
Landing marker: A filled red circle (radius 10px) at −12. Label below: "Land: −12" in red text. Appears only after all 3 jumps are complete.
Below the number line: Three sequential buttons — "Jump 1 →", "Jump 2 →", "Jump 3 →". Each activates only after the previous jump animation completes (approx. 600ms each). A running position display: "Current position: ___".
Result box (purple, bottom of screen): Appears after Jump 3 completes — "3 × (−4) = −12 | Positive × Negative = Negative".
Top-right note: A small label "← Leftward = negative direction" with a thin arrow pointing left.
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
3 × (−4) means: start at 0 and take 3 jumps of 4 steps to the LEFT. "3 × (−4) ka matlab: 0 se shuru karo aur 4 kadam ki 3 chhalaangein baayi taraf lagao."(3 × (−4) means: start at 0 and take 3 jumps of 4 steps to the left.)
Tap "Jump 1" to take the first step to the left. "Pehli chhalaang lagao — 0 se −4 par pahuncho."(Take the first jump — go from 0 to −4.)
After 3 jumps, we land on −12.

So: 3 × (−4) = −12
Positive × Negative = Negative ✓		 "Teen chhalaangon ke baad −12 par pahunche. Isliye: Positive × Negative = Negative!"(After three jumps we landed on −12. Therefore: Positive × Negative = Negative!)
Interaction Detail
  • Student
    Taps "Jump 1 →" button.
  • System
    Arc 1 draws from 0 to −4 (stroke-dashoffset animation, 600ms). Position counter: "Current position: −4". "Jump 2 →" activates.
  • Student
    Taps "Jump 2 →".
  • System
    Arc 2 draws from −4 to −8. Counter: "Current position: −8". "Jump 3 →" activates.
  • Student
    Taps "Jump 3 →".
  • System
    Arc 3 draws. Red landing circle appears at −12. Counter: "Current position: −12". Result box fades in. "Continue →" activates.
Feedback Logic
Guided — No wrong path
Buttons are locked in sequence — only the current jump button is active. No incorrect tap scenario is possible on this screen. A "Replay jumps" outline button appears after completion so students can rewatch the animation before continuing.
4
The Big Discovery — Negative × Negative = Positive
Pictorial → Abstract
Page Overview
Aha! Moment

This is the heart of the lesson. Students extend the pattern table using a fixed negative multiplicand (−4) and a changing first number (3 down to −3). The pattern forces the result of (−1)×(−4) to be +4 — students discover this themselves rather than being told.

Visual Description
Same table format as Screen 2. Title: "Now we keep (−4) fixed and change the first number." Second factor column always shows (−4).
Top half (rows 1–4) — pre-filled, coral fill: 3×(−4)=−12, 2×(−4)=−8, 1×(−4)=−4, 0×(−4)=0. Sign pills show − Negative for rows 1–3, grey "zero" for row 4. These fade in automatically.
Bottom half (rows 5–7) — green fill, locked: (−1)×(−4), (−2)×(−4), (−3)×(−4). Product and Sign cells are blank/greyed. Revealed by student prediction.
Right-side arrow changes direction: Now points UPWARD (green) alongside the green rows — "Products increase by +4 each step ↑".
Row 5 — (−1)×(−4) — highlighted: Double green border (2px), subtle background pulse. This is the "moment" row.
Below the full table: A green observation box (fades in after all rows are revealed): "The pattern would break if (−1)×(−4) were negative! So it MUST be +4. Negative × Negative = Positive."
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
Now (−4) is fixed. We change the first number from 3 down to −3. Watch the products carefully. "Ab (−4) fixed hai. Pehle number ko 3 se −3 tak badlate hain. Products par dhyan do."(Now (−4) is fixed. We change the first number from 3 to −3. Pay close attention to the products.)
Products: −12, −8, −4, 0 ...

The products are going UP by 4 each time now!		 "−12, −8, −4, 0 — ab products har baar 4 se badh rahe hain! Toh (−1) × (−4) kya hoga?"(−12, −8, −4, 0 — now the products are increasing by 4 each time! So what will (−1) × (−4) be?)
If (−1) × (−4) were −4, the pattern would break!

So it MUST be +4.
Negative × Negative = Positive ✓		 "Agar answer −4 hota, toh pattern toot jaata! Pattern ko theek rakhne ke liye answer +4 hona chahiye. Negative × Negative = Positive — yahi prove hua!"(If the answer were −4, the pattern would break! To keep the pattern consistent it must be +4. Negative × Negative = Positive — this is what has been proved!)
Interaction Detail
  • System
    Top half (coral rows) fade in automatically one by one, 300ms apart, while voiceover plays.
  • System
    Fill-in box appears below row 4: "(−1) × (−4) = ___".
  • Student
    Types the predicted product and taps "Check".
  • System
    If correct (+4): Row 5 glows green, reveals fully. Upward arrow appears. Voiceover plays the Aha! line. "Reveal Next" button appears.
  • Student
    Predicts and reveals (−2)×(−4) and (−3)×(−4) in the same way.
  • System
    After all three green rows are revealed, the observation box slides up from the bottom. "Continue →" activates.
Feedback Logic
Attempt 1 — Wrong
"Upar ki rows ka pattern dekho — product badh raha hai ya ghatt raha hai?"
(Look at the rows above — is the product increasing or decreasing?)
The upward green arrow pulses to direct attention to the increasing trend.
Attempt 2 — Wrong
"0 × (−4) = 0 tha. Ab 4 aur jodo." (0 × (−4) = 0. Now add 4 more.)
Show the scaffold: "0 + 4 = ___" for the student to complete. This concretises the upward step.
Attempt 3 — Wrong
Auto-reveal +4 with the full Aha! animation and voiceover. No penalty — this is a discovery moment, not an assessment. Proceed automatically to "Reveal Next" for the remaining rows.
Correct on Any Attempt
Green burst animation on the revealed row. "Excellent! You found it!" toast (2 seconds). The observation box slides in immediately for reinforcement.
5
Sign Rules — All Four Cases in One View
Abstract Stage
Page Overview
Concept Consolidation

Present all four sign-rule combinations in one memorable 2×2 visual grid. Students tap each card to see a worked example. A single mnemonic rule ties everything together and prepares them for practice.

Visual Description
2×2 grid of cards fills the centre of the screen. Each card is a large rounded rectangle (~270px wide, ~170px tall).
Card 1 — top-left, green: Large text: "(+) × (+)". Below a thin divider: "= (+)" in bold. Bottom tag: "e.g. 3 × 4 = 12".
Card 2 — top-right, coral: "(+) × (−)" | "= (−)" | "e.g. 3 × (−4) = −12".
Card 3 — bottom-left, coral: "(−) × (+)" | "= (−)" | "e.g. (−3) × 4 = −12".
Card 4 — bottom-right, green with a glowing pulsing border: "(−) × (−)" | "= (+) !" in bold green | "e.g. (−3) × (−4) = +12". This card has a repeating subtle glow pulse (1.5px green outer shadow, 2s cycle) to mark it as the special discovery case.
Below the grid — amber mnemonic banner: "Same signs → Positive (+)  |  Different signs → Negative (−)".
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
There are only 4 cases in integer multiplication. Here they all are — in one place. "Integer multiplication mein sirf 4 cases hote hain. Yeh sab ek hi jagah hain."(In integer multiplication there are only 4 cases. They are all in one place here.)
Tap any card to see a worked example inside it. "Kisi bhi card ko tap karo — ek example andar dekhoge."(Tap any card — you will see a worked example inside it.)
Remember this one rule:
Same signs → Positive. Different signs → Negative.		 "Ek rule yaad rakho: same signs hain toh answer positive. Alag signs hain toh answer negative."(Remember one rule: if signs are the same the answer is positive. If signs are different the answer is negative.)
Interaction Detail
  • System
    Cards appear with staggered fade-in (Cards 1 → 2 → 3 → 4, 250ms apart). Card 4 has an extra glow pulse on entry to draw the eye.
  • Student
    Taps any card.
  • System
    Card scales up slightly (1.05×) and a step-by-step worked example appears inside: e.g. for Card 4 — "(−3) × (−4): Same signs → Positive → 3 × 4 = 12 → Answer: +12". A "Got it ✓" mini-button collapses the card.
  • Student
    After reviewing all four (or as many as desired), taps "Continue →" at the bottom.
Feedback Logic
Exploratory — No wrong path
No right-or-wrong interaction on this screen. Students freely tap cards. The goal is familiarity with all four cases before the practice phase begins on Screen 7.

Error Bridge — Fixing the Most Common Mistake
Misconception Bridge
Page Overview
Error Bridge

Before independent practice, directly address the most common student misconception — thinking that negative × negative = negative. This screen shows the wrong thinking, exposes it using the pattern, and clearly corrects it. It resets the student's mental model before they attempt problems on their own.

Visual Description
Split-screen layout — left and right panels side by side.
Left panel (pale red, 1px red border, rounded): Header "Common Mistake ✗" in red. Below: a cartoon student face (simple, neutral) with a thought bubble: "Both numbers are negative → answer must be negative?" A wrong answer displayed in large red text: "(−3) × (−4) = −12" with a diagonal red crossed-out line through it.
Centre: A bold purple arrow pointing right, labeled "But the pattern says..."
Right panel (pale green, 1.5px green border, rounded): Header "What the Pattern Shows ✓" in green. A compact 3-row pattern table: 0×(−4)=0, (−1)×(−4)=+4, (−2)×(−4)=+8. A green upward arrow: "+4 each step ↑". Final line in bold green: "(−3) × (−4) = +12 ✓".
Below both panels: The mnemonic banner (amber): "Same signs → Positive. Different signs → Negative."
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
Many students think: both numbers are negative, so the answer must be negative. This is the most common mistake. "Bahut se students sochte hain: dono numbers negative hain, toh answer bhi negative hoga. Yeh ek bahut common galti hai."(Many students think: both numbers are negative so the answer must also be negative. This is a very common mistake.)
But our pattern table has already proved: (−3) × (−4) = +12, not −12. "Lekin hamara pattern table prove kar chuka hai: (−3) × (−4) = +12 hai, −12 nahi."(But our pattern table has already proved: (−3) × (−4) = +12, not −12.)
Rule: Same signs → Always Positive.
Different signs → Always Negative.		 "Rule yaad rakho: same signs hone par answer HAMESHA positive hoga. Alag signs par HAMESHA negative."(Remember the rule: when signs are the same the answer is ALWAYS positive. When signs are different it is ALWAYS negative.)
Interaction Detail
  • System
    Left panel fades in first (400ms). The wrong answer and crossed-out animation play automatically.
  • System
    After 2 seconds, the "But the pattern says..." arrow animates (draws left to right). Right panel fades in.
  • Student
    Can tap the compact pattern table on the right to expand it to full size for review.
  • Student
    Taps "I understand — let me practise" button (green, bottom centre) to proceed to Screen 7.
Feedback Logic
Explainer — No student input required
This screen plays automatically. The only student action is tapping "I understand" to continue. No branching or wrong-answer path exists. Purpose: clear the misconception before independent practice, not to assess.

Guided Practice — Apply the Sign Rule
Interactive Workspace
Page Overview
Practice

Students solve three problems independently with adaptive scaffolding that activates only when they make errors. Problems increase in difficulty: Q1 is a direct (−)×(−) case, Q2 uses mixed signs, Q3 is a three-factor extension. A "Hint" button is always available but not pushed.

Visual Description
One question at a time. Progress indicator at the top: three dots — ● ○ ○ → ● ● ○ → ● ● ●
Question card: Purple fill, white text, centred on screen. Large readable font (20px). Below it: a text entry box (or 4-option MCQ for Q1) with a "Check" button (green, right of box). A "Hint" button (amber, outline) sits below — visible but not highlighted.
Top-right score counter: "⭐ 0 / 3" — fills one star per correct answer.
Q1: "(−6) × (−5) = ?"   Q2: "(−8) × 7 = ?"   Q3 (Extension): "(−3) × (−3) × (−3) = ?"
Correct confirmation: Green toast at bottom ("Correct! ✓") and one star fills in the score counter. Next question card slides in from the right.
Scripting
On-Screen Text (English)Voiceover — Hindi + English
Q1: Find (−6) × (−5).

Step 1: Check the signs. Step 2: Multiply the numbers. Step 3: Apply the rule.		 "Pehle signs check karo, phir numbers multiply karo, phir sign rule lagao."(First check the signs, then multiply the numbers, then apply the sign rule.)
Q2: Find (−8) × 7.

Are the signs the same or different?		 "Signs same hain ya alag? Wahi decide karega ki answer positive hoga ya negative."(Are the signs the same or different? That will decide whether the answer is positive or negative.)
Q3: (−3) × (−3) × (−3) = ?

Tip: Multiply any two numbers first. Then multiply the result by the third.		 "Teen numbers hain — pehle koi bhi do multiply karo, phir us result ko teesre se."(There are three numbers — first multiply any two, then multiply that result by the third.)
Interaction Detail
  • Student
    Q1: Types or selects answer, taps "Check". Expected: +30.
  • System
    If correct: green toast, one star fills. Q2 card slides in from the right.
  • Student
    Q2: Types answer, taps "Check". Expected: −56.
  • System
    If correct: green toast, second star fills. Q3 card slides in.
  • Student
    Q3 (Extension): A two-step work area appears — "Step 1: (−3) × (−3) = ___" then "Step 2: ___ × (−3) = ___". Student fills both boxes.
  • System
    If all correct (Q3 answer = −27): Three stars animate one by one. Brief confetti burst (green dots). "Excellent! You mastered it!" full-screen toast for 2 seconds. "Continue →" activates.
Feedback Logic
Q1 — Attempt 1 Wrong
"Signs kaisi hain — same ya different?" (What are the signs — same or different?)
The sign rule mnemonic banner slides up from the bottom of the screen as a reference.
Q1 — Attempt 2 Wrong
"Dono negative hain — same signs. Toh answer positive hoga. Ab 6 × 5 = ___"
(Both are negative — same signs. So answer will be positive. Now calculate 6 × 5 = ___)
Show two-step scaffold on screen: "Step 1: Signs same → Answer is + | Step 2: 6 × 5 = ___".
Q1 — Attempt 3 Wrong
Auto-demonstrate full solution with animation: "(−6) × (−5) → Same signs → Positive → 6 × 5 = 30 → Answer: +30". Voiceover narrates each step. Proceed to Q2 automatically.
Q2 — Attempt 1 Wrong
"Signs alag hain — toh answer negative hoga. Ab 8 × 7 karo."
(Signs are different — so the answer will be negative. Now calculate 8 × 7.)
Q3 — Attempt 1 Wrong
"Pehle sirf pehle do numbers lo: (−3) × (−3) = ?"
(First take only the first two numbers: (−3) × (−3) = ?)
Highlight Step 1 box only. Step 2 box is greyed out until Step 1 is correct.
All 3 Correct
Three stars animate one by one (500ms apart). Confetti burst (green dots, 1.5 seconds). Toast: "You have mastered integer multiplication!" Score displayed: 3/3.

Developer and Designer Implementation Notes
Hindi rendering: Use Noto Sans Devanagari (Google Fonts, free licence). All Hindi must appear in Devanagari script in the production build — do not use transliterated Roman script.

Colour system: Green (#5a9e3a / #edf6e5) = positive/correct. Coral (#d4572e / #fdf0ec) = negative/error. Purple (#6b5fc0 / #f0eefc) = abstract concepts and neutral frames. Amber (#c98a10 / #fef8eb) = hints and mnemonics.
Animations: Row reveals → fade-in (300ms ease). Number line arcs → SVG stroke-dashoffset (600ms ease-in-out). Card entry → slide-up + opacity (250ms ease). No bounce or spring physics. Keep pacing calm and classroom-appropriate.

Tap targets: Minimum 44×44px on all interactive elements (WCAG AA compliance). Essential for tablet use in classroom environments.

Voiceover: Record in a natural, teacher-style Hindi voice at approximately 130 words/minute. Provide bilingual subtitle tracks (Hindi Devanagari + English). Do not use AI TTS for the production version.
